Self-supervised classification of variable stars

SAMM Lab, Université Paris 1 · 2026, ongoing · Supervised by Alain Celisse

Problem. Variable stars (e.g. Cepheids, RR Lyrae, eclipsing binaries) are classified from photometric light curves — brightness measurements taken at irregular intervals over months or years. Standard supervised classifiers require large labelled datasets, which are expensive to produce for new surveys.

Method. We adapt contrastive self-supervised learning to Selective State Space Models (Mamba), which handle irregular sampling natively without interpolation. The encoder is pre-trained on unlabelled OGLE-IV light curves via a contrastive objective, then fine-tuned with a small labelled set.

Current state. Pre-training pipeline in PyTorch. Evaluating representations on the OGLE-IV benchmark (Udalski et al.) against baseline RNN and Transformer encoders.

Key references: Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2023) · SimCLR (Chen et al., 2020) · GRU-ODE (De Brouwer et al., 2019) · Raindrop (Zhang et al., 2021)

Jump detection in asset prices via sequential FDR control

Ongoing · 2026

Problem. High-frequency asset price processes exhibit occasional large discontinuities (jumps) driven by news arrivals, liquidity shocks, or microstructure effects. Reliably separating jumps from continuous Brownian variation requires testing thousands of intraday intervals simultaneously — a multiple-testing problem that naive thresholding handles poorly, inflating false discoveries under market stress.

Method. We frame jump detection as an online multiple-testing problem and work entirely in the e-value framework. At each intraday interval a local e-value is constructed from a bipower-variation or truncated-variance statistic; e-values compose multiplicatively and remain valid under arbitrary stopping and dependence, making them natural for sequential price data. FDR is controlled via the e-BH procedure (Wang & Ramdas 2022) and online variants such as e-LOND, without requiring a p-value conversion that would distort the test under heavy-tailed returns.

Current state. Simulation study on Bates and Merton jump-diffusion models. Benchmarking power and FDR against the Lee-Mykland and Aït-Sahalia-Jacod statistics.

Key references: e-BH procedure (Wang & Ramdas, 2022) · e-values: calibration & properties (Vovk & Wang, 2021) · Lee-Mykland jump test (2008) · Aït-Sahalia & Jacod (2009)

Past

EU Consolidated Tape reconstruction

EuroCTP, Amsterdam · Jun–Aug 2025 · Internship

Context. MiFID II fragmented European equity trading across dozens of venues, destroying the consolidated view of best prices that a centralised exchange would provide. MiFIR Article 22a mandates a Consolidated Tape Provider (CTP) for equities; EuroCTP responded to the ESMA tender in 2025.

Work. Built an event-driven pipeline in Python/PySpark that reconstructs the EU equity consolidated tape by merging time-stamped trade and quote feeds from multiple venues (Euronext, CBOE Europe, Turquoise, dark pools). The pipeline quantified inter-venue timestamp desynchronisation biases that affect EBBO (European Best Bid and Offer) computation. Findings fed into the technical annex of EuroCTP's ESMA submission.

Tools. Python, PySpark, SQL, Clustering. Data: BMLL Data Lab.
